What This Is

Interactive Role-Play Training

This tool simulates real phone interviews an adjudicator conducts when gathering information on unemployment insurance claims.

Who You Are

An Unemployment Insurance Adjudicator

You are conducting an official fact-finding interview to gather information needed to adjudicate an unemployment insurance claim.

How It Works

When you start the conversation, you'll choose to speak with either the claimant (employee) who filed for unemployment benefits, or the employer whose account is affected by the claim. Each party has been given a distinct persona and backstory.

Conduct your interview as you would in a real fact-finding call. Take notes, ask follow-up questions, and try to gather all the information you'd need to make a fair determination.

👤

Claimant Interview

  • Full name, address, phone, and email
  • Employer's legal name (from W-2), address, and phone
  • First and last day of work
  • Reason for separation from employment
  • Any additional jobs worked in the last 18 months
🏢

Employer Interview

  • Contact's name and title
  • Legal business name and account number
  • Whether the employee separated from employment
  • Date of separation and circumstances
  • Any documentation or policy violations
⚠ Training Note — Realistic Difficulty

Both the claimant and employer personas have been deliberately designed to be challenging to interview. The claimant may be emotional, evasive, or confused about dates and facts. The employer may be defensive, uncooperative, or vague about the reason for separation. This reflects the real-world complexity adjudicators encounter daily — where getting complete, accurate information requires patience, professional persistence, and skillful questioning. Practice navigating these dynamics in a safe environment before applying them on live cases.
